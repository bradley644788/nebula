About Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun, and as of yet, it is the only astronomical object known to contain life. It is one of the four terrestrial planets within our Solar System, meaning it is characterised by a solid surface and composed of silicate rocks and metals. Earth is an ocean world, being the only planet in the Solar System to be able to sustain liquid surface water, almost all of which is contained within the global ocean, covering 70.8% of Earth's crust. The percentage remainder is occupied by land. Earth's crust consists of slowly moving tectonic plates, which have over time interacted to form mountain ranges, but also landforms like volcanoes, and natural disasters like earthquakes.
